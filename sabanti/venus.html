VENUS

Venus is the second planet from the Sun. It is the second largest terrestrial object of the Solar System. Just slightly smaller in size and mass than Earth, having a substantial atmosphere and by neighbouring Earth, Venus is sometimes called Earth's "sister" or "twin" planet. Orbiting the Sun allways closer than Earth makes it (like Mercury) allways appear in Earth's sky in inferior position, meaning close to the Sun, and at night only as "morning star" or "evening star". It furthermore appears in Earth's sky as the brightest natural object, aside from the Sun and Moon, due to its proximity to Earth and the Sun, its large size and high albedo.[19][20] This prominent appearance of Venus in Earth's sky has made Venus a common and prime object in human culture and astronomy, with its planetary motion across the sky being tracked as early as the second millennium BCE.
